What is Depop?

Depop is a social shopping app that marries the posting facet of Instagram with the selling aspects of Poshmark.
Depop was founded in 2011 and allows sellers to post pictures of their trendy clothes for sale in a timeline format that is similar to Instagram.
Depop has over 15 million users with the mass majority of them being under the age of 25.

Selling on Depop

Pros

  • Listing Fees
    • Much like Mercari, Depop charges their sellers zero listing fees whenever they post an item.
      This allows sellers to post as much as they want in order to increase their sales and ultimately bring more buyers to their page.
    • Depop does have a 10% comission fee that is applied to all sales.
  • Easy to Find Clothing
    • Finding great deals on clothing is easy to find on Depop if you are a buyer.
      Depop has a tremendous search engine that allows users to search by brand,size,color,and by hashtag.
  • Ease of Access
    • If you are familiar with using Instagram, then you will have no issue navigating Depop.
      Because Depop modeled their platform after the most popular app in history, this has helped boost their number of users throughout time.

Cons

  • Too many Dropshippers
    • Depop has entirely too many dropshippers that congest the clothing feeds with stock images of cheap clothing.
      This tends to bury the posts of real clothing and tarnishes the Depop brand of being a trendy clothing selling app.
  • Replica Clothing
    • With the over abundance of dropshippers comes the posting of replica clothing and accessories.
      Depop is flooded with fake jackets,coats,handbags,backpacks,and jewlry.
      This causes caution within buyers and affects how they interact with sellers who have genuine merchandise to sell.
  • Shipping
    • Shipping on Depop is a NIGHTMARE.
      Depop has zero regulations when it comes shipping and it causes strife among both the buyers and sellers.
      Depop offers their own flat rate shipping up to one pound in weight but it is very expensive at $9.99.
      Depop sellers are encouraged to ship on their own and update the tracking number whenever the item is sent.
      Because there is no true guidelines for shipping, sellers can ship items whenever they want and it cause animosity between the selling community and the buyers community.
      There have also been reports that sellers will accept payment from a buyer and then never ship the item.
      These types of incidents tend to draw people away from an ecommerce site.
